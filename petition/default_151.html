Day 12: Free The Arctic 30

The Arctic 30 are facing two months behind bars in Russia for a peaceful protest against dangerous oil drilling.

They took action on September 18 at the giant Prirazlomnaya oil rig, where Gazprom intends to become the first company to pump oil from icy Arctic waters.

Over a day later the ship was illegally boarded by armed special forces, before being towed to Murmansk.

28 activists, as well as a freelance videographer and a freelance photographer, have been refused bail.

Greenpeace International executive director Kumi Naidoo has stated, "The Arctic is melting before our eyes, and these brave activists stand in defiance of those who wish to exploit this unfolding crisis to drill for more oil."

Breaking: Several of the Arctic 30 are now officially charged with piracy, which carries a maximum sentence of 15 years. More charges expected soon.
